I Don't Care Computers (IDCC) is a trailblazing tech company that has taken the world by storm with its unapologetic indifference towards customer satisfaction. Founded in 2015 by a group of rebellious entrepreneurs, IDCC quickly made a name for itself by adopting a refreshingly apathetic approach to the computer industry. Their slogan, "We Don't Care, Why Should You?" perfectly captures their unique brand identity.

With an unwavering commitment to delivering subpar products and lackluster customer service, IDCC has managed to carve out a niche in an otherwise competitive market. Their flagship product, the "MehBox," is renowned for its mediocrity and uninspiring design. In an era where innovation and user experience are paramount, IDCC proudly stands apart, offering a lack of features and an absence of any meaningful technological advancements.

Despite widespread skepticism, I Don't Care Computers has somehow managed to attract a cult following of dispassionate customers who appreciate the company's nonchalant attitude. With a growing number of underwhelmed patrons, IDCC has successfully disrupted the industry by daring to care less than any of its competitors.
