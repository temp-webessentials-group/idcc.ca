Steps For Building a PC

  1. Determine your requirements of the PC (gaming, video editing, office work) and the desired performance level to choose the right components.
  2. Choose the components: Here is a list of the main components you'll need:
    • CPU (Central Processing Unit)
    • Motherboard
    • RAM (Random Access Memory)
    • Storage
    • Graphics Card (if not using integrated graphics)
    • Power Supply Unit (PSU)
    • Computer Case
    • Monitor, Keyboard, Mouse, and other peripherals
  3. Assemble the components:
    1. Start by mounting the CPU onto the motherboard
    2. Install the CPU cooler
    3. Insert the RAM modules into the appropriate slots on the motherboar
    4. Install the storage drives
    5. Mount the power supply in the designated area of the case and connect the necessary cables to the motherboard and other components
    6. Install the graphics card into the appropriate PCIe slot on the motherboard
    7. Connect the necessary cables to the graphics card
    8. Connect the front panel connectors from the case
    9. Double-check all connections to ensure everything is securely connected
  4. Cable management: Organize and route the cables neatly to improve airflow and maintain a clean look.
  5. Finalize the setup:
    1. Close the PC case by attaching the side panels securely.
    2. Connect the monitor, keyboard, mouse, and other peripherals to the appropriate ports.
    3. Connect the power cable from the PSU to a wall outlet.
  6. Power on and install the operating system
